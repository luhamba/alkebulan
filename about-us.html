'CONNECTING AFRICAN ARTISTS TO THE WORLD'

THE PROBLEM

The problem that exists is that African art constitutes to less than 1% of the global arts market despite the wealth of talent that exists. It is estimated that 60% of Africa’s population is below the age of 25 years with an abundance of talent among them with creative skills in the arts.

 

These challenges have been exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic which has disrupted supply chains across the world. In Zambia, the pandemic caused a reduction in flights at international airports as passenger movements by aviation reduced by more than 70.6%. The pandemic upended the industry because foreign tourists accounted for majority of sales for contemporary art and which reduced dramatically thereby threatening livelihoods.

 

In addition, local artists have limited access to markets to grow their craft which has led to low revenues and reduced public financing towards the sector. A reason for the latter is the argument that the arts and crafts don’t sufficiently contribute to economic growth and development. Therefore, there is a need to explore innovative ways of exposing African art to the world.

OUR SOLUTION

Our solution is to the problem is through Alkebulan which is a social enterprise built to;

 

1. Spread our culture through ‘Social Hubs’ which promote skills development in contemporary arts

 

2. Our virtual gallery which enables local artists to auction their work by providing them with access to global markets

 

3. Enable artists to take ownership of their work through royalties via use of block-chain and Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs)

 

The industry has the potential to add-value to the lives of local artists, their communities and the economy at large though increased revenues. This will be done exposing our local industry to the world which will allow our culture to live and thrive.
